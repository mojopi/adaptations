Semester Azul is immersive, mixed-age, integrated semester-long program taking place at High Tech High in the spring of 2019. In the course, three adults and twenty-five teenagers are spending fifteen weeks doing work around our local marine environment. We hope to kayak, snorkel, scuba dive, film, photograph, and innovatively collect data informing us about the physical, chemical and biological status of the local water to help us tackle difficult questions and dilemmas around the systems and ethics at play.

Currently, we're particularly interested in creating a kind of artificial reef that serves as both an underwater lab and marine restoration project. In addition to working on creating this small scale reef, individuals will be doing individual projects around this restoration worksuch as making an data-collecting buoy, and producing a short documentary film.

Semester Azul is inspired by a similar course run in the spring of 2018 called Semester Upstream.
